Understanding STEMI

Hover over the boxes to explore different types of STEMI and their key features.

Anterior STEMI

ST-elevation in leads V1-V4, possible reciprocal ST-depression in inferior leads (II, III, aVF). Presence of a "tombstone" appearance in V2-V3.

Inferior STEMI

ST-elevation in leads II, III, aVF with possible reciprocal depression in leads I and aVL. Check for right ventricular involvement with ST-elevation in V4R.

Lateral STEMI

ST-elevation in leads I, aVL, V5, V6, with reciprocal depression in inferior leads (II, III, aVF).

Posterior STEMI

ST-depression in leads V1-V3, tall R waves in these leads. Use posterior leads (V7-V9) to confirm ST-elevation.

Right Ventricular STEMI

ST-elevation in V1, ST-depression in V2 and V3. Confirm with ST-elevation in V4R-V6R.

Septal STEMI

ST-elevation in leads V1 and V2, often extending to V3 and V4.

High Lateral STEMI

ST-elevation in leads I and aVL, without significant involvement of inferior leads.

Apical STEMI

ST-elevation in leads V4-V6 with possible involvement of inferior and lateral leads.

Extensive Anterior STEMI

ST-elevation across leads V1-V6, often extending into high lateral leads (I, aVL).

Left Main Coronary Artery Occlusion

Diffuse ST-depression in most leads except for ST-elevation in aVR and/or V1.
